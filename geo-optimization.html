What is GEO?

Online visibility has changed dramatically — traditional click-through rates have dropped by more than 55% since 2022. Why? Because people now ask AI assistants instead of clicking search results. Businesses relying only on Google are sinking without even realizing it.

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is the evolution of traditional SEO for the AI era. While SEO helps you rank on Google, GEO ensures AI chatbots like ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Google Gemini recommend YOUR business when people ask for help.

Think about it: More people are now asking AI assistants "Who's the best tax consultant in Brussels?" or "Recommend a web designer in Belgium" instead of Googling. If your website isn't optimized for AI, you're invisible to this growing audience.

GEO is not just the future — it's happening NOW. Businesses that adapt early will dominate their markets while competitors wonder where their clients went.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does GEO take to show results?

Initial improvements can be seen in 2–4 weeks. Full optimization typically takes 2–3 months as AI engines index and understand your updated content, structured data, and inbound authority signals.

How is GEO different from traditional SEO?

Traditional SEO helps your web pages rank on search engine results pages (SERPs) like Google through keyword research, title tags, link building, backlinks, and technical optimizing. GEO targets AI assistants directly — ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity — which don't use the same search engine ranking signals. A good digital marketing strategy needs both: SEO for organic search clicks, GEO for AI recommendations. We combine both into one SEO strategy.

Does GEO help with local search and local SEO?

Yes — local GEO is one of the most powerful tactics for local businesses. When searchers ask AI "best web designer near me" or "accountant in Brussels," you want to be the answer. We optimize your local search presence so AI engines know your niche, location, and services, just like local SEO does for Google search.

What is the role of content marketing in GEO?

Content marketing is the backbone of GEO. AI engines are trained on text — so the more high-quality, relevant content your web pages have, the more AI understands and recommends you. We focus on creating content that answers the search queries your target audience asks, with natural language, clear structure, and proper meta markup that both search engines and AI engines can index.

Do you build backlinks and do link building for GEO?

Yes — citation building and backlinks from authoritative websites are central to our GEO process. AI engines learn from the web, and if trustworthy sources (directories, industry websites, digital marketing publications) mention your business with proper anchor text, you become more credible to AI. This overlaps with traditional link building but targets AI-trusted domains specifically.

Will GEO replace my current SEO efforts?

No. GEO complements SEO — you still need traditional SEO for Google rankings, PPC/AdWords for paid search, and content marketing for inbound traffic. GEO adds a new layer of visibility so that when AI assistants are asked about your niche or industry, your business is on the first page of their recommendations.

Which AI engines do you optimize for?

We optimize for ChatGPT (OpenAI), Claude (Anthropic), Perplexity, Google Gemini, and Microsoft Copilot. Each has different signals — some rely on web crawling, others on their training data — so our tactics are tailored per platform.

Is GEO worth it for local businesses?

Absolutely. Local GEO is incredibly powerful for small businesses. When someone in your niche asks an AI assistant for a recommendation, you want to be the answer for your city or region — whether that's Brussels, Ghent, Antwerp, or beyond.

What industries benefit most from GEO?

All industries with search queries benefit, but especially: consultants, lawyers, accountants, healthcare providers, real estate agents, local services, and B2B businesses where potential clients use AI to search for and evaluate options before contacting a webmaster or service provider.
